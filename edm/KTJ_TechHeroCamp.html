Learning how to code and build apps

Kids are growing up in a very different world now. Smartphones, computers, YouTube, and Facebook are embedded in their daily lives. Even toys are digital, and many are programmable, such as Lego Mindstorms and the new-generation microcontrollers, Arduino.

If students are taught biology and mathematics in order to understand the world around them, then knowing the basics of how computers communicate should also be given.

Coding is one way to do it. Coding (also called programming) is telling a computer, app, phone or website what you want it to do. Some educators and experts are calling it the 'new language', an essential skill for kids to express themselves in this age and time. Once they pick up the basic of coding skills, they will be able to build useful applications, games and websites.

Beyond the practical reasons for learning how to code, there's the fact that creating a game, app or animation can be really fun for kids
